How I started taking care of myself

The last years of my adolescence were quite troubling. I suffered from some sort of headache that stopped me from focusing, studying. There was always a heat feeling in my head. I saw the best doctors in my country. Nothing wrong was found in electroencephalograms and other brain tests. I got a break when someone suggested that I worked out regularly and mainly to put my head upside down with my body against a wall. I would wake up in the morning and practiced aerobics on TV before going to school.

This whole chapter took a toll on me. At 4 feet 11 inches and around 90 pounds, I was tiny, picky in everything, including food. One day, I realized I was killing myself and decided to feed myself properly. By that time, I used to earn my own money tutoring young kids. I had the means to buy myself whatever foods I wanted.

After high school and a secretarial certificate, I got a job, continued to follow closely my nutrition and intermittently paying a gym membership.

Moving to the States did not stop me from monitoring my physical wellbeing. However, life is different here. Foods are different. They are more available not necessary healthier which makes it more difficult to evaluate. Moreover, with the passing years, my body has been less receptible, and my fat genes have been prevailing. As a result, in other to stay fit and healthy, I have to pay more attention to my daily eating habits, my daily physical activities and daily lifestyle habits in general. I keep them simple and it is working

.


Basics to Healthy Eating

My first approach to healthy eating is planning ahead: Not only have at hand the basics for any meal from fruits to vegetables, to whole grains, to roots, to spices, to supplements powder such as collagen but also owning the necessary cooking utensils, small appliances, food storage containers preferably glasses. Planning saves time and money and keeps unhealthy foods away.

The list might look excessive and foolish. It is neither one.
This is a life investment similar to any investment, to the same level as buying cars, clothes, and houses.
Eating poorly destroy the body and makes it impossible to enjoy anything in life.


I.- Ready To Use Items Such As:


  • Fruits
  • Spices And Herbs
  • Whole Grains
  • Beans
  • Vegetables and Roots
  • Nuts
  • Cereals
  • Frozen seasoned meats or fish

II.- Utensils and Appliances

  • A steamer is very practical. Vegetables, whites and sweet potatoes, roots such as yucca, yam, to name it can be steamed without over-cooking or any extra-added unhealthy ingredients or species. I own a Uniware one. I got it since 2004. It is very strong and spacious. Unfortunately, it's not available on the market anymore.
  • A Pressure Cooker saves a lot of time with hard to cook items such as dry beans. I also use it for cow feet, which contain gelatin

  • Having a few specific Sizes of Pans And Pots makes it easy to stick to specific routines. For example, the best way to get a hot herbal tea first thing in the morning is assigning a pot to that use and having the tea ready on the stove and rewarm it. The best way get mines is shopping around discount stores.

  • A Single Serve Blender is the on-the-go portion control item for smoothies, puree, green juice etc. Mine is a Hamilton Beach, a very good brand. It is very quiet. It can be easily used even at work or in a space shared between roommates .

  • A Regular Size Blender definitely, is required for cooking. I have an Hamilton Beach one. This brand lasts forever.

  • When it comes to juicing, having an A Juicer is a must.

  • A Mixer makes it easy to mix ingredients. Eating healthy does not mean banning baking foods in our life. With everything one click away on the web, baking our own treats, becomes easy and gives a rewarding feeling. The plus side is knowing, choosing our own ingredients.

  • Whether or not the running water provided by the Utility Company is safe to use, having a Water Filter for some household activities such as soaking fruit and vegetables is an extra layer of safety. It is wiser to rinse the triple-washed spinach bag before juicing it; better safe than sorry.

  • A large Colander is very useful to clean and soak fruit and vegetables.



III.- Storage and Containers

  • Glass or Window Stainless Containers allow regular and quick inventories. They avoid food waste.

  • Storage for Cooked Foods is another phase in a good eating plan. Eating healthy does not mean cooking every day. Whole or partial meals can and should be frozen in glass containers preferably. Nowadays there is no time to cook from scratch every day. By having something frozen and ready, the urge to call for takeout can be contained.


  • Handling a food supply requires not only containers but also storage cabinet. Healthy food Supplies should be accessible at any time not lost in the back of any cabinet in the house. Sliding-doors cabinets and wall mounted shelves for kitchen are useful to that end. They do not take up too much space.

  • Portable Water Bottles are available in all shapes, sizes and materials and used for hot or cold beverages. No need to buy an expensive coffee, hot chocolate, green juice and water every day; bring them from home and save money.

IV.- And the Ultimate Item

  • As foolish as it sounds, a separate Freezer, the size and shape to be decided, can turn out very helpful in any household. Supplies of cooked foods, leftovers, frozen fruits and vegetables, and even grains should be properly stored. Sometimes, the top part of the household fridge does not have enough room for everything.
